## Solo adjustment (2026-07-08) — takes precedence over sections 6 and 12

Decision: we run solo.

Going solo removes nothing essential. The plan was already reusable-artifact-driven and
one-person-feasible. Two collaborator-driven arms are now cut: GRN propagation, which was
weakly differentiated because the group's own LLCB already computes direct and indirect
effects; and model fine-tuning, which was off the critical path and does not beat baselines.

Sharpened headline: reversal is not enough. Ranking perturbations that reverse or phenocopy a
disease signature is a released, commodity capability and the obvious move. On its own it
nominates phenotype-but-toxic targets. An essential gene whose knockdown is cytotoxic also
collapses an inflammatory signature, so it looks like a hit. Our contribution is the
safety-gated therapeutic window that separates genuinely actionable targets from these false
positives. We validate it two ways: by recovering approved drugs with reported precision and
recall, and by a head-to-head that shows which targets a naive reversal ranking nominates that
our window-and-safety screen rejects as common-essential or homeostasis-collapsing.

What this changes versus sections 6 and 7:

- The headline is the method and the safety correction, not a single disease. This keeps the
  entry distinctive even though the two obvious disease frames are crowded.
- Do not showcase STAT6 or GATA3. The known master regulators are the crowded, obvious picks.
  Feature instead the naive-versus-safe head-to-head, and novel, non-obvious candidates that
  the window-and-safety triage elevates. The degrader-handle axis stays as an annotation, with
  STAT6 and the oral degrader KT-621 cited only as the validated archetype for the axis, not as
  our finding.
- Optionally demonstrate generality across both an autoimmune and an allergy signature, to show
  the method is not tuned to one disease. The differentiator is the rigor and the safety gate
  applied across the activation axis, not the disease choice.

Demo spine: first the benchmark figure recovering approved drugs; second the naive-versus-safe
head-to-head, the reversal-is-not-enough moment; third two or three evidence cards for novel,
safety-passing, druggable candidates.

## 1. One-line thesis

The first benchmark-validated, context-selective therapeutic-window target triage over the
genome-scale CD4+ T cell Perturb-seq atlas. It scores every knockdown for suppression of a
pathogenic activation program specifically in stimulated cells while sparing rest,
homeostasis, and viability; validates by recovering approved immunomodulator targets; layers
druggability, degrader tractability, and disease genetics; adds an in-silico on-target safety
readout from the knockdown transcriptome; and ships a ranked shortlist as reproducible,
sourced evidence cards. It is showcased on a druggable intracellular type-2 network case study.

## 2. Competitive landscape and what is actually novel

The competition check is the decisive input. Seven months after the dataset posted, no
external group has done target-triage, therapeutic-window, or signature-reversal work on it.
The citing works are the same consortium's resource papers, perturbation-prediction
benchmarks, or GRN work on other data. No challenge or leaderboard is attached to it.

Prior art we must cite and build on, never claim as novel:

- pert2state (Zhu, Dann 2025 repo): a released ElasticNet that ranks the regulators which
  reconstruct any target-state signature. This means "signature to regulator" and
  "phenocopy a disease signature" are largely a solved, released capability.
- Weinstock et al. 2024, Cell Genomics: causal GRN inference (LLCB, direct and indirect
  effects) in CD4+ T cell perturbations, plus sLDSC and ABC heritability, GWAS fine-mapping,
  and a drug-target nomination (KMT2A). So GRN-plus-immune-genetics on these cells is already
  published by this group. GRN propagation is weakly differentiated. The genetics-annotation
  layer is not a standalone differentiator.
- The 2025 authors themselves: autoimmune GWAS enrichment via Open Targets (Figure 7), Th1/Th2
  polarization regulator nomination (Figure 4), aging regulators (Figure 5), and a full
  released toolkit.

The genuinely open, defensible contribution, occupied by none of the above:

1. Context-selectivity reframed as a therapeutic window. The authors describe context-specific
   regulators at the cluster level. No one triages targets by a therapeutic window, meaning
   active in the disease-relevant stimulated state and quiet at rest.
2. A drug-target-recovery benchmark on this atlas. Recovery-of-known-targets is an established
   validation principle in general, but there is no formal precision/recall evaluation of a
   perturb-seq perturbation ranking against an approved-drug-target gold standard.
3. Druggability and degrader tractability fused into the ranking. The paper uses zero
   druggability or therapeutic language. A degrader-handle axis, tied to the oral STAT6
   degrader milestone, is entirely unoccupied here.
4. In-silico on-target safety from the knockdown transcriptome. No paper names or benchmarks it.
5. An agentic, Claude-Science-driven deliverable: sourced per-target evidence cards and the
   multi-agent literature synthesis behind them.

## 3. Why this is scientifically valuable

- It converts a descriptive atlas into a decision. The value of a screen for drug discovery is
  not the regulator list; it is a defensible, ranked, annotated shortlist a lab can act on.
- It is causal, not correlational. Every claim is "knocking this down does X in this context,"
  the correct framing for translating loss of function to inhibition or degradation.
- It is falsifiable. The benchmark reports precision and recall against known targets, including
  where the score fails. That is a number, not an opinion.
- It is reusable. The scorer, the benchmark harness, and the evidence-card generator transfer to
  other perturbation atlases and other diseases.

## 4. Why it fits the Researcher track

The brief asks for a discrete, reproducible output: a finding, a trained model, or an analysis
others can reproduce, using Claude Science. We deliver all three: a validated scoring pipeline
(a model), a ranked druggable target shortlist (a finding), and a one-command-reproducible
repository (an analysis). Claude Science is visible throughout: the parallel literature review,
the ground-truth curation, the genetics and druggability annotation, and the evidence cards.

## 6. Disease-frame decision (recommendation)

The main open choice is the disease frame. The dataset is non-polarized culture, so its true
strength is the Rest-versus-Stim activation axis, not polarization. Recommendation:

- Build and validate the method on the broad activation-inflammatory program. This is
  data-native: the therapeutic window is literally suppress the stim-induced program, spare
  rest. It is autoimmune-relevant and uses the dataset's real signal.
- Showcase a focused type-2 (STAT6/GATA3) degrader case study for the demo. It is timely (oral
  STAT6 degrader KT-621), translational, and striking, without betting the whole thesis on the
  weaker non-polarized Th2 signal.

This is frame-robust. It serves an autoimmune-leaning or a type-2-leaning collaboration equally,
and it hedges the non-polarized limitation by leading with the broad program and treating type-2
as a case study, not the headline claim.

## 7. Method (grounded in reusable artifacts)

Inputs. `GWCD4i.DE_stats.h5ad` (per-perturbation x per-condition; layers log_fc, zscore,
adj_p_value; confidence columns guide_correlation, donor_correlation, single_guide_estimate;
QC flags offtarget_flag, low_target_gex). No reprocessing of raw cells.

1. Pathogenic program. Score each perturbation against a pathogenic activation module with
   decoupler run directly on the effect matrices. Define the module from inflammatory cytokines
   and Th1/Th17 markers. In parallel, run pert2state (the authors' released model) against an
   external disease signature (IBD or atopic dermatitis) for a signature-reversal view. Two
   complementary program definitions strengthen the signal and subsume the state-reversal idea.
2. Therapeutic-window score. Reward context-selective suppression, a strong Stim effect with a
   near-zero Rest effect. Penalize loss of homeostasis and tolerance and induction of
   stress/apoptosis. Add a viability penalty from DepMap common-essential and Hart CEG2, the
   latter already bundled in the authors' metadata. Normalize by a within-condition null,
   an E-distance-style signal-to-noise. Gate by donor and guide concordance using the released
   confidence columns.
3. Benchmark (the headline). Recover approved immunomodulator targets from the ranking. Positive
   sets: Pharos Tclin, ChEMBL max_phase=4, and the curated immunomodulator table in the review.
   Report AUROC, top-k enrichment odds ratio, and precision/recall. Report failures too.
4. Annotation. Druggability and tractability from Open Targets (including PROTAC buckets), Pharos
   TDL, and ChEMBL. A degrader-handle axis: Open Targets PROTAC bucket, canSAR or fpocket
   ligandability on AlphaFold models, existing-degrader flags from PROTAC-DB and MolGlueDB, and
   CRBN/VHL availability in CD4+ cells. Genetics by reusing the authors' released OpenTargets
   autoimmune outputs plus GWAS Catalog, FinnGen, and OneK1K, building on Weinstock 2024.
5. Safety axis. In-silico on-target safety from the knockdown transcriptome: collateral
   disruption of core-essential and housekeeping programs, and a homeostasis-preservation score
   in resting cells. Anchor with Hart CEG2, DepMap, and GTEx or Human Protein Atlas breadth.
6. Type-2 case study. Run the full pipeline against a Th2-collapse objective, phenocopying
   STAT6/GATA3, and show the degrader-handle ranking surfaces a druggable intracellular type-2
   network beyond STAT6.
7. Deliverables. A ranked target table, 5 to 10 sourced evidence cards generated agentically,
   the benchmark figure, and a one-command-reproducible repository.

## 8. What we reuse versus what we build

Reuse (already released): the DE effect matrices; the pert2state model; Hart core-essential and
OpenTargets autoimmune gene lists bundled in the authors' metadata; th1_th2_known_regulators;
external reference screens (Schmidt, Freimer, FOXP3); pertpy, decoupler, pydeseq2, crispat.

Build (the contribution): the therapeutic-window composite score; the drug-target-recovery
benchmark harness; the degrader-handle and safety-liability axes; the agentic evidence-card
generator; the reproducible one-command pipeline.

Feasible because so much is reusable.

## 10. Risks and honest limitations

- Non-polarized data. Th2 and Th17 signals are proxies read within stimulated cells. Lead with
  the broad program; treat type-2 as a case study.
- Pseudobulk, not distribution-aware. We inherit the released pseudobulk DE. State it.
- The gold standard is imperfect. Report where the benchmark fails, not only where it wins.
- The degrader-handle score is a prior, not proof of degradability. Word it humbly.
- Single-gene library. No combinatorial perturbations.
- Model temptation. Keep foundation and perturbation models off the critical path; the
  literature shows they do not beat simple baselines. Geneformer zero-shot deletion is the only
  optional, baseline-gated side arm.

## 11. Novelty positioning: what to cite versus what to claim

Cite as precedent: pert2state and the authors' polarization, aging, and autoimmune analyses;
Weinstock 2024 (GRN plus genetics, KMT2A); signature reversal (Lamb, Sirota, Chen); selective
essentiality (DepMap, Freimer); the PROTACtable genome (Schneider); recovery-of-known-targets
validation (Dong/Ye, Frangieh, Fang).

Claim as contribution: the context-selective therapeutic-window composite; the first formal
drug-target-recovery evaluation of a perturb-seq ranking; the fused degrader-handle axis; the
in-silico safety-from-knockdown-transcriptome readout; and the agentic evidence-card deliverable.
