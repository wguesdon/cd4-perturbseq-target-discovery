## What was tested

The project headline is that ranking perturbations purely by how strongly they suppress an
inflammatory program nominates phenotype-but-toxic targets, and that a safety gate is what
separates real targets from those false positives. That claim was scheduled for Day 3. It was
pulled forward to Day 1 because everything else depends on it, and it was written so it could
fail: the script exits non-zero if the naive ranking is no more toxic than background.

Naive score, per perturbed gene, in `Stim48hr`:

    suppression = -mean(zscore over the 32 measured effector-core genes)

The baseline was steelmanned rather than strawmanned. Standard QC was applied first, dropping
`distal_offtarget_flag`, `neighboring_gene_KD`, and `low_target_gex`, and requiring
`ontarget_significant`. That takes 11,281 stimulated perturbations to 6,371. The claim is
therefore that even a competently QC'd reversal ranking is toxic.

Every enrichment was retested against a background matched on `n_cells_target` decile, because
perturbations assayed in more cells have more power, and power alone would produce both more
extreme z-scores and more significant DE genes.

## The verdict: the bet holds

Against the cell-count-matched background, the top 100 are enriched for:

| Liability | Top 100 | Matched background | Test | p |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Inborn error of immunity (IUIS) | 14.0% | 4.0% | Fisher, OR 3.91 | 0.012 |
| Collateral DE genes in Stim | median 356.5 | median 5.5 | Mann-Whitney | 2.4e-17 |
| DE genes at Rest (homeostasis) | median 70.0 | median 4.0 | Mann-Whitney | 2.6e-09 |
| Tolerance-module suppression | median 1.09 | median 0.21 | Mann-Whitney | 8.1e-14 |

The unmatched background gives the same answer with larger effect sizes, and the score is in
fact *negatively* correlated with cell count (Spearman -0.109), so power is not manufacturing
the result.

## The prediction that failed

The strategy predicted the contaminants would be **common-essential genes**. They are not.

Zero of the top 100 are core-essential. That on its own proves nothing, because the Fisher test
had no power: only 31 core-essential genes survive QC out of 6,371 rankable perturbations, so
the expected count in a top 100 is about 0.5. The informative test is where those 31 actually
rank.

    median rank of the 31 rankable essentials:  3148 of 6371
    median rank of everything else:             3188 of 6371
    Mann-Whitney (essentials ranked better):    p = 0.611

Dead centre. The naive score is indifferent to core essentiality. The authors' Hart subset also
under-samples the library: 283 symbols, of which only 68 are perturbed at all and 64 tested in
`Stim48hr`.

Caveat stated plainly: with 31 rankable essentials we can exclude a strong enrichment, not a
weak one. What we can say is that a viability penalty anchored on Hart CEG2 or DepMap
common-essentials, which is what the strategy specified, would have rejected almost none of the
naive ranking's false positives.

## What the contaminants actually are

Reading the top 25 gives two clear classes.

**Immune-essential signalling machinery.** `STAT5B` (rank 1), `VAV1` (2), `IL2RB` (4),
`CD3G` (14), `CD247` (15), `LCK` (24), `RBCK1` (3), `INO80` (11). These are the TCR and IL-2
signalling core. Knocking them down does suppress the inflammatory program, completely, and in
humans loss of function of these genes causes immunodeficiency, not remission. They are not
essential to a cancer cell line. They are essential to a T cell. `IL2RB` is the sharpest case:
`IL2RA` is a validated drug target (basiliximab), while `IL2RB` loss of function is severe
combined immunodeficiency.

**Global transcriptional machinery.** `NSD1`, `TADA2B`, `CXXC1`, `WDR82`, `USP22`, `SUPT20H`,
`MED13`, `UBR4`, `DHX36`, `BRD1`. These carry 2,000 to 5,300 DE genes in stimulated cells and,
damningly, 1,700 to 4,700 DE genes **at rest**. They do not suppress the inflammatory program
selectively. They collapse the transcriptome, and the inflammatory program goes down with it.

Both classes also suppress the tolerance module (`FOXP3`, `IL10`, `IKZF2`, `CTLA4` and the
co-inhibitory checkpoints) along with the effector module, which is the therapeutic-window
failure in its purest form.

## Consequence for the method

This changes the safety gate, and it makes it more defensible rather than less.

The gate must not be anchored on cancer-cell essentiality. The axes that actually separate the
false positives here are:

1. **Immune essentiality.** IUIS inborn-errors-of-immunity genes, as a human genetic
   loss-of-function readout. This is orthogonal to DepMap and directly on-target for the
   question being asked.
2. **Resting-transcriptome disruption.** DE-gene count at Rest, which is the operational form
   of "spare homeostasis" and needs no external database at all. It is measured in the screen.
3. **Tolerance-module preservation.** Suppressing `FOXP3`, `IL10`, and the checkpoints is a
   liability, not a benefit, even though those genes are stimulation-induced.

Axes 2 and 3 are computed entirely from the knockdown transcriptome. That is precisely the
"in-silico on-target safety readout" the strategy claimed as a contribution, and it is now
motivated by a result rather than asserted. Hart CEG2 stays in as a reported column, because
`DHFR` is both a real methotrexate target and core-essential, and the benchmark should show that
tension rather than hide it.
